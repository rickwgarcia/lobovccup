# Frequently Asked Questions

---

## General Questions

**What is the Lobo VC Cup?**
The Lobo VC Cup is UNM's student startup investment competition. Student-run VC teams pitch real LPs for capital, then deploy that capital into student-founded startups. It's the most realistic venture capital simulation available to undergrads — because it's not a simulation.

**Who can participate?**
Any current UNM student. Both tracks (Founder and VC) require at least one registered UNM student per team. Teams can include students from any school or major.

**Do I have to be a business student?**
Absolutely not. The best teams are often cross-disciplinary. Engineers, scientists, pre-med students, artists — all of them have competed and won. Your major doesn't matter. Your hustle does.

**Is there a fee to participate?**
No. Participation is free.

**Can I participate in both tracks?**
No. You must choose one track — Founder or VC — at registration. You cannot be on a Startup team and a VC team simultaneously.

**How many people can be on a team?**
- Founder teams: 1–5 members
- VC teams: 2–6 members

**Can I be on a team with people from outside UNM?**
Your team must include at least one current UNM student. Non-UNM members are allowed but must still register through the portal.

## Founder Questions

**Do I need a working product to compete?**
No. Pre-revenue, pre-product startups have competed and raised capital. What matters is the quality of your idea, your team, and your pitch. That said, traction helps — a lot.

**What if I don't have a team?**
Solo founders can register. The portal will have a co-founder matching feature closer to registration. Check back closer to the competition.

**What type of startups can compete?**
Any type. Tech, biotech, consumer, social enterprise, hardware, SaaS — it's open. The only requirement is that it's your original work.

**What does "founders win what they raise" mean?**
There is no fixed first/second/third place prize for founders. Instead, startup teams receive actual investment capital from VC teams. If three VC teams invest in your startup, you receive the sum of those investments. The best startups typically raise the most.

**Will my pitch deck be shared with people outside the competition?**
Your deck is only visible to registered VC teams and competition administrators. It is not shared publicly.

**What do I need to bring to Discovery Days?**
Your pitch deck (on a laptop or tablet), yourself, and your energy. Printed one-pagers are a nice touch but not required. Show up ready to pitch dozens of times over two nights.

**What happens if a VC team wants to invest?**
They'll submit a term sheet on Investment Day (March 30). You can accept, decline, or negotiate. You're under no obligation to accept any offer.

## VC Questions

**How much capital do we actually deploy?**
The LP panel awards capital to VC teams based on LP pitch quality. The amount is real and will be announced during Education Week. All awarded capital is intended to be invested into student startups.

**What makes a good fund thesis?**
Specificity and conviction. A thesis isn't "we invest in innovative companies." A thesis is "we invest in hardware startups founded by engineering students solving manufacturing inefficiencies in New Mexico's industrial sector." The more specific your lens, the more credible your sourcing strategy.

**Do we have to invest in all the startups?**
No. Investment is entirely at your discretion. You could invest in one company or seven. You could pass on all of them (though you'll need to defend that choice in your portfolio summary).

**What's in a deal memo?**
A deal memo is a 1–2 page internal document explaining why you're making — or passing on — an investment. It should cover the company, the team, the market, your diligence findings, and your investment recommendation with reasoning.

**What format should term sheets be in?**
A Word or PDF document is fine. We're evaluating the quality of the terms and your reasoning, not legal formatting. Use the term sheet structure from Education Week as your baseline.

**What if we can't agree on a term sheet with a founder?**
That's a real outcome in venture capital. Document the breakdown in your portfolio summary and explain what happened. Judges understand negotiations fall apart.

**Are we graded on whether our portfolio companies "succeed"?**
No. You're graded on the quality of your process — how you sourced, evaluated, structured, and documented your investments. You can't control outcomes. You can control rigor.

## Logistics

**Where is Discovery Days?**
Mesa Computing Module (MCM), Room 1010. On the main UNM campus in Albuquerque.

**Where is Investment Day?**
Johnson Student Center (JSC), main UNM campus.

**Where is the Awards Ceremony?**
Johnson Student Center (JSC), main UNM campus.

**Are virtual options available for in-person events?**
No. Discovery Days, Investment Day, and the Awards Ceremony are in-person only. These events require physical presence. Plan accordingly.

**What if I miss a mandatory event?**
Teams that miss mandatory in-person events (Discovery Days and Investment Day) may be disqualified. Contact the organizers immediately if you have an emergency.

## Technical / Portal Questions

**Why does registration require a @unm.edu email?**
Competition eligibility requires UNM affiliation. The portal enforces @unm.edu email addresses at signup.

**I submitted the wrong file. Can I re-upload?**
Yes. The portal allows re-uploads before the submission deadline. Your most recent upload is what gets evaluated.

**I'm having trouble logging in.**
Try resetting your password from the login page. If the issue persists, email delcampo@unm.edu with the subject line "Portal Access Issue."
