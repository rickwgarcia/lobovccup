# Founder Pathway Guide

Welcome to the Lobo VC Cup Founder Track. You're here to raise real capital from your peers. Here's exactly how it works.

---

## Overview

Startup teams pitch student-run VC funds during Discovery Days. The VC teams evaluate startups, conduct due diligence, and decide whether to invest. The best startups raise the most. There are no judges picking winners — just investors making bets.

---

## Requirements

- At least one team member must be a current UNM student
- Teams can have 1–5 members
- Startups must be at pre-seed or seed stage
- Your idea must be original (no class projects resubmitted without significant development)
- All team members must register at lobovccup.unm.edu

---

## The Founder Timeline

### Education Week — March 2–6 (Virtual)
Kick off the competition with live virtual workshops covering:
- How to craft an investor-ready pitch deck
- What VCs look for at the pre-seed stage
- Term sheets and investment basics
- Q&A with past Lobo VC Cup participants

**Attendance is strongly recommended.** These sessions are designed to give you a real edge.

Register your team at lobovccup.unm.edu. You'll need:

- Brief idea description (1–2 paragraphs)

### Pitch Prep Week — March 9–12 (Virtual)
Work directly with mentors and advisors to sharpen your pitch. Office hours are available — book them. This is your chance to stress-test your deck before the real thing.

### Pitch Deck Submission — Due March 13, 11:59 PM
Upload your pitch deck (PDF or PPTX) to the submissions portal. No extensions. Be ready to present from this deck at Discovery Days.

**Your deck should cover:**
- The problem you're solving
- Your solution
- Market size (TAM / SAM / SOM)
- Business model
- Traction (users, revenue, pilots, LOIs)
- Team and why you
- What you're raising and how you'll use it

### Discovery Days — March 25–26 (IN-PERSON)
**Location:** Mesa Computing Module (MCM), Room 1010
**Time:** 5:00–7:00 PM

This is the main event. VC teams will rotate through startup booths. You'll pitch 5–7 minute presentations followed by Q&A. Multiple VC teams may choose to invest. The best startups get multiple term sheets — which means leverage.

**Bring:**
- Printed one-pagers (optional but recommended)
- Laptop or tablet for your deck
- Business cards if you have them
- Energy — this is your moment

### Investment Day — March 30 (IN-PERSON)
**Location:** Johnson Student Center (JSC)
**Time:** 6:30–8:30 PM

Final investment decisions are made. VC teams present term sheets to startups. Negotiations happen. Capital gets deployed.

### Awards Ceremony — April 3 (IN-PERSON)
**Location:** Johnson Student Center (JSC)
**Time:** 11:30 AM – 1:00 PM

VC prize winners are announced. Investment totals are revealed. Celebrate with the full Lobo ecosystem.

## How to Build a Strong Pitch Deck

**Slide 1 — Title**
Company name, one-line description, your name and role.

**Slide 2 — Problem**
Make us feel the pain. Real customer quotes help. Data helps more.

**Slide 3 — Solution**
Your product or service, clearly explained. Include a screenshot or mockup if possible.

**Slide 4 — Market Size**
TAM, SAM, SOM. Show your work. Don't guess — cite sources.

**Slide 5 — Business Model**
How do you make money? Unit economics if you have them.

**Slide 6 — Traction**
Users, revenue, pilots, letters of intent, waitlist signups. Any proof of demand.

**Slide 7 — Competition**
Who else is solving this? Why are you different? Don't say "we have no competition."

**Slide 8 — Team**
Why are you the team to do this? Relevant experience, domain expertise, unfair advantages.

**Slide 9 — The Ask**
How much are you raising? What will you use it for? What milestones will it hit?

**Slide 10 — Contact**
Email, website, LinkedIn.

## FAQs for Founders

**Do I need a working product?**
No, but the further along you are, the better your chances. Traction talks.

**Can I pitch an idea I'm just starting?**
Yes. Pre-idea teams have competed and raised. Just be honest about where you are.

**What if multiple VCs want to invest?**
Great problem to have. You'll negotiate terms and can take investment from multiple VC teams or choose your preferred lead.

**What happens after the competition?**
Investments made during Lobo VC Cup are for the competition. However, many connections made here turn into real mentorships and follow-on opportunities within the UNM ecosystem.
